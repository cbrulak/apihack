The Library of Congress was destroyed in a fire lit by the British in the War of 1812. At the time, the personal collection of President Thomas Jefferson was considered to be the most comprehensive library in the country. In order to restore the national library, Jefferson sold all 6,487 of his books to the Library of Congress in 1815. The library has now grown to more than 120 million items and over 74 terabytes of data freely available to the public, and is centered by the Thomas Jefferson Building.

In the spirit of the contribution made by Jefferson, this site aims to bolster the tools already available on the Library of Congress website for searching its database of historical images.
